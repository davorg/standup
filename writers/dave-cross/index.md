![Dave Cross](https://davecross.co.uk/img/dc-cartoon.png)

Dave Cross has been on the fringes of the London comedy scene for over forty
years.

In 1982, he was the social secretary for The City University. He was one of
the first social secretaries to start booking what we used to call
"alternative comedy" shows at the Students Union - booking acts like
John Hegley, Otiz Cannelloni and Norman Lovett.

In the late 90s, he became known as "Computer Dave" amongst a group of
London stand-ups. He developed the first websites for Ed Byrne, Jeff Green
and John Moloney, set up Jeremey Hardy's first home internet connection
and persuaded Don Ward that the Comedy Story really needed a website.

Around the turn of the millennium, he was part of an informal drinking
club that had coalesced around the upstairs bar at the Bedford in Balham,
and would often be there into the early hours, chatting with comics who
had performed at the Banana Cabaret.
