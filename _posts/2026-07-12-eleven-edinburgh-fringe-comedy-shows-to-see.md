The Edinburgh Fringe programme is large enough to make choosing shows feel
like a full-time job. I will only be in Edinburgh for the start of this year's
festival, so the scheduling may yet become brutal, but these are eleven of the
comedians currently at the top of my list.

This is not an attempt at a definitive guide to the best comedy at the 2026
Fringe. It is a personal list: some proven favourites, some people I have been
meaning to see for years, and a conspicuous number of performers whose
profiles have been transformed by the arrival of *Saturday Night Live UK*.

## Ahir Shah: *Golden*

I loved Ahir Shah on *The Mash Report*, where his combination of intelligence,
anger and tightly controlled exasperation always felt perfectly suited to the
state of the news. His previous show, *Ends*, won the Edinburgh Comedy Award.
In [*Golden*](https://www.edfringe.com/tickets/whats-on/ahir-shah-golden),
he turns to love, money, family, responsibility, fear, forever and, apparently,
frogs. That is quite a list for an hour, but Shah is one of the acts I trust to
make it cohere.

## Ania Magliano: *Peach Fuzz*

A year ago, I am not sure I could have told you who Ania Magliano was. Now,
after *Taskmaster* and *Saturday Night Live UK*, I am wondering whether I will
even manage to get a ticket. [*Peach Fuzz*](https://www.edfringe.com/tickets/whats-on/ania-magliano-peach-fuzz)
promises material about her body, floatation tanks, Sabrina Carpenter and the
Suffragettes. Magliano has already had three acclaimed sell-out Fringe runs;
this feels like the year the wider audience catches up.

## Kristen Schaal: *The Legend of Crystal Shell*

This one sounds completely bonkers, which is very much part of the appeal.
Kristen Schaal's new comedy play is in Edinburgh for only five nights. She
plays a woman hiding from the world because of a fantastic secret, preparing
to realise her dream of performing a solo dance interpretation of
Tchaikovsky's *Swan Lake*. It co-stars her *Bob's Burgers* colleague John
Roberts. Read the official description of
[*The Legend of Crystal Shell*](https://www.edfringe.com/tickets/whats-on/kristen-schaal-the-legend-of-crystal-shell)
and tell me you are not at least curious.

## Larry Dean: *Hellbent*

Larry Dean was already a three-time Edinburgh Comedy Award nominee, but
*Saturday Night Live UK* has introduced him to a much larger audience.
[*Hellbent*](https://www.edfringe.com/tickets/whats-on/larry-dean-hellbent)
finds him recently married but still refusing to grow up. Dean has an unusually
warm, high-energy presence and a formidable gag rate. This may be another
ticket that has become considerably harder to obtain since March.

## Nish Kumar: *Angry Humour From a Really Nice Guy*

Another *Mash Report* alumnus, and another obvious choice for me. The title
[*Angry Humour From a Really Nice Guy*](https://www.edfringe.com/tickets/whats-on/nish-kumar-angry-humour-from-a-really-nice-guy-work-in-progress)
is a neat summary of Kumar's comic persona. This is a work in progress about
inequality, autocracy and political collapse. So, light holiday entertainment.
Political comedy can date at terrifying speed, but a work-in-progress hour
from Kumar should be an excellent way to take the temperature of this very
strange year.

## Olga Koch: *Fat Tom Cruise*

I have somehow never seen Olga Koch live, and I do not have a good explanation
for that. [*Fat Tom Cruise*](https://www.edfringe.com/tickets/whats-on/olga-koch-fat-tom-cruise)
is billed as an immersive, genre-defying show built around one good story that
is not really hers. That description is deliberately cryptic, but Koch's track
record suggests the formal ambition will be matched by actual jokes. It is
time I finally saw her in a room rather than on a screen.

## Paddy Young: *Will Sir Be Laughing Alone?*

Paddy Young is another performer whose visibility has leapt through
*Saturday Night Live UK*, where he shared the Weekend Update desk with Ania
Magliano. His new show,
[*Will Sir Be Laughing Alone?*](https://www.edfringe.com/tickets/whats-on/paddy-young-will-sir-be-laughing-alone),
looks at love, loneliness and his impressive record of fumbling romantic
opportunities. He was nominated for Best Newcomer in 2023; this should be a
good chance to see what the post-SNL version of his stand-up looks like.

## Jack Dee: *Jack's Joke Show*

The irresistible phrase here is **"Guaranteed no new material."** After 40
years in stand-up, Jack Dee has assembled his favourite old stories, jokes and
anecdotes into
[*Jack's Joke Show*](https://www.edfringe.com/tickets/whats-on/jack-dee-jack-s-joke-show).
At a festival obsessed with the new, a veteran comic openly promising the
opposite feels almost radical. It also sounds like an extremely dependable
way to spend an hour.

## Ayoade Bamgboye: *Small Talk*

Ayoade Bamgboye won Best Newcomer at the 2025 Edinburgh Comedy Awards and then
joined the inaugural *Saturday Night Live UK* cast. Her 2026 appearance is a
work in progress with a Q&A, built around a defence of the much-maligned art
of small talk. [*Small Talk*](https://www.edfringe.com/tickets/whats-on/ayoade-bamgboye-small-talk-wip-q-a)
is therefore both a chance to see what follows an award-winning debut and an
opportunity to hear a new show being worked out in public.

## Lara Ricote: *INKLING*

Lara Ricote is another comedian I have intended to see for years without ever
quite managing it. She won Edinburgh's Best Newcomer award in 2022. Her
description of [*INKLING*](https://www.edfringe.com/tickets/whats-on/lara-ricote-inkling)
begins with a man being mean to her in a museum and expands into a show about
saluting the mystery of being alive. It calls itself her stupidest and
smartest show yet. I am sold.

## Rose Matafeo: *Work in Progress Morning Hour*

Rose Matafeo completes the group of performers I should have seen live by
now. Her early work-in-progress show does not yet claim to know what it is
about. Instead, its official listing aims for the relaxed atmosphere of the
museum café in *Animal Crossing*, where a pigeon serves coffee while a dog
plays guitar. That is a remarkably specific vibe, and exactly why
[*Work in Progress Morning Hour*](https://www.edfringe.com/tickets/whats-on/rose-matafeo-work-in-progress-morning-hour)
has made the list.
